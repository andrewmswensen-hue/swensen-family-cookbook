*Kaori Allen*

**Classic Pumpkin Scones**

*Sally's Baking Addiction*

2 cups all-purpose flour

2.5 tsp baking powder

1 tsp ground cinnamon

1.5 tsp pumpkin pie spice

½ tsp salt

½ cup butter, frozen

1/3 c + 2 Tbl heavy cream, **divided**

1 large egg

½ cup canned pumpkin puree, blotted

½ cup light brown sugar

1 tsp vanilla extract

course sugar for top (optional)

**Maple Glaze**

2 Tbl unsalted butter

1/3 cup pure maple syrup

1 cup sifted powdered sugar

pinch salt, to taste

Preheat oven to 400 degrees, rack to middle-low position. Line baking
sheets with parchment.

**Scones:** Whisk first five ingredients in a large bowl. Grate the
frozen butter. Add to dry ingredients and combine with a pastry cutter
or fingers until in comes together in pea-sized crumbs. Set aside.

Whisk the rest of the ingredients together in a small bowl. Drizzle over
the flour mixture and mix until everything appears to be moistened.

With floured hands, work it into a ball and transfer to a floured
surface (will be really sticky). Make one 8-inch disk and divide into 8
wedges OR divide into two 5-inch discs and cut each into 8 wedges using
a very sharp knife.

Place 2" apart on prepared pans. Use a pastry brush to brush scones with
2 Tbl heavy cream and sprinkle with course sugar if desired.

Large scones -- bake on one pan for 20-25 minutes or until lightly
browned.

Smaller scones -- bake 18--20 minutes or until lightly browned on two
pans.

Remove from oven and let slightly cool.

**Glaze**:

In a small saucepan over low heat, melt the butter and syrup together,
whisking occasionally. Once the butter has melted, remove from heat and
whisk in the powdered sugar. Taste and add a pinch of salt if desired.
Drizzle over the warm scones.

Best served warm or will keep for up to 2 days. Can freeze unglazed for
up to 3 months. Thaw overnight then warm and glaze them.

*If you're on a diet don't even LOOK at this recipe!! These are

**Dough:**

2 Tbl active dry yeast

1 tsp sugar

1 cup warm water (105 -- 115 deg)

1 cup warmed milk

1/3 cup sugar

1/3 cup melted butter

2 tsp salt

2 eggs, slightly beaten

6-8 cups all-purpose flour, more if needed (start with 6)

**Filling:**

1 cup melted butter, divided

1¾ cup granulated sugar, divided

3 Tbl ground cinnamon

1½ cups chopped walnuts (optional)

1½ cups raisins (optional)

**Creamy Glaze:**

1/3 cup melted butter

4 cups powdered sugar

2 tsp vanilla

4-8 Tbl hot water

**To prepare dough:** In a small bowl mix together warm water, yeast,
and 1 tsp sugar; set aside. In a large bowl mix milk, 1/3 cup sugar,
melted butter, salt and eggs; stir well. Add yeast mixture and mix well.
Add half of the flour and beat until smooth. Stir in enough of the
remaining flour until dough is slightly stiff (dough should be sticky.)

Turn onto a well-floured board; knead 5-10 minutes. Place in a
well-buttered glass or plastic bowl, cover, and let rise in a warm
place, free from drafts, until doubled in bulk, about 1 - 1½ hours.

When doubled, punch down dough and let rest 5 minutes. Roll out on a
floured surface into a 15\" x 20\" rectangle.

**To prepare filling:** Spread dough with ½ cup melted butter. Mix
together 1½ cups sugar with cinnamon; sprinkle over buttered dough.
Sprinkle with nuts and raisins if desired. Roll up jelly-roll fashion
and pinch edge together to seal. Cut into 12 - 15 slices.

Coat bottom of a 13\" x 9\" baking pan and an 8\" square pan with
remaining ½ cup butter and sprinkle with the ¼ cup sugar. Place cinnamon
roll slices close together in pans. Let rise in a warm place until dough
is doubled in bulk, about 45 minutes.

Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Bake 25-30 minutes, or until rolls are
nicely browned. Cool rolls slightly.

**To prepare glaze:** As rolls are cooling, in a medium bowl mix melted
butter, powdered sugar, and vanilla. Add hot water 1 Tbl at a time until
glaze reaches desired spreading consistency. Spread over slightly cooled
rolls.

**Overnight Cinnamon Rolls**

*Makes 12*

2 ¼ tsp yeast (1 packet)

2 Tbl warm water

1 cup milk, warmed but not hot

¼ cup sugar

1 egg, beaten

3 ½ cups flour

¼ tsp salt

6 Tbl melted butter

Combine yeast and warm water in a bowl. Stir and let dissolve. Pour milk
over this and stir. Add sugar and egg and stir. Add **2 cups** flour and
salt. Mix well by hand. Add remaining 1 ½ cups flour. Mix until sticky,
but dough pulls away from side into a ball.

**Fried Chicken Sandwiches**

*Food Network*

¾ tsp cayenne

1 ¼ cup buttermilk

4 brioche or pretzel hamburger buns

1/3 cup pickle juice

16 dill pickle slices

2 Tbl hot sauce

kosher salt and fresh ground black pepper

4 boneless skinless chicken thighs

veg oil for frying

1 c vegetable shortening

2 cups self-rising flour

1 ½ tsp paprika

1 tsp dry mustard

Combine pickle juice, 1Tbl hot sauce, and ½ tsp salt in a large
re-sealable bag. Add chicken, seal bag, and shake to evenly disperse.
Refrigerate and marinate for at least 2 hours or up to overnight. When
ready to fry remove chicken from the bag and pat dry. Discard the
marinade.

Fill a large heavy pan with 2 ½ inches of oil then add the shortening.
Bring to 350 degrees over medium heat (use a thermometer). Line a baking
sheet with paper towels and set aside.

Whisk together the flour, paprika, mustard, cayenne, ½ tsp salt and ¾
tsp pepper in a large bowl. Pour in ¼ cup of the buttermilk and use your
fingers to work it in, making the flour shaggy. Whisk remaining
buttermilk and 1Tbl hot sauce in another large bowl.

Dip a piece of chicken in the buttermilk, drip off excess, dredge in the
flour mixture, pressing it in to fully coat. Set on a plate and repeat.
If the chicken gets soggy, dredge again in the flour mixture.

Once oil is at 350 degrees, slide in chicken and turn off the heat,
allowing the oil to lower to 300 degrees. Turn heat back on to low and
fry chicken, turning as needed to get chicken browned, adjusting heat to
keep it at 300 degrees. Fry until chicken is a deep golden brown and 170
degrees inside. Remove to lined trays and sprinkle with a bit of salt.
If working in batches, bring oil back up to 375 for each new batch and
repeat.

Split the buns and toast. Place 4 pickle chips and hot chicken between.

2 cups heavy cream

2 medium cloves garlic, minced

1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves, roughly chopped

Kosher salt and black pepper

4 to 4 ½ pounds russet potatoes, peeled and sliced 1/8-inch thick on a
mandolin slicer (7 to 8 medium, see note)

2 tablespoons unsalted butter

PREPARATION

Adjust oven rack to middle position and heat oven to 400 degrees.
Combine cheeses in a large bowl. Transfer 1/3 of cheese mixture to a
separate bowl and set aside. Add cream, garlic and thyme to cheese
mixture. Season generously with salt and pepper. Add potato slices and
toss with your hands until every slice is coated with cream mixture,
making sure to separate any slices that are sticking together to get the
cream mixture in between them.

Grease a 2-quart casserole dish with butter. Pick up a handful of
potatoes, organizing them into a neat stack, and lay them in the
casserole dish with their edges aligned vertically. Continue placing
potatoes in the dish, working around the perimeter and into the center
until all the potatoes have been added. The potatoes should be very
tightly packed. If necessary, slice an additional potato, coat with
cream mixture, and add to casserole. Pour the excess cream/cheese
mixture evenly over the potatoes until the mixture comes halfway up the
sides of the casserole. You may not need all the excess liquid.

Cover dish tightly with foil and transfer to the oven. Bake for 30
minutes. Remove foil and continue baking until the top is pale golden
brown, about 30 minutes longer. Carefully remove from oven, sprinkle
with remaining cheese, and return to oven. Bake until deep golden brown
and crisp on top, about 30 minutes longer. Remove from oven, let rest
for a few minutes, and serve.

Time: 80min

2 cups chopped onions

2 tablespoons chopped Italian parsley

1 tablespoon chopped fresh sage

1 tablespoon chopped fresh rosemary

1 tablespoon chopped fresh thyme

1 teaspoon salt

1/2 teaspoon black pepper

8 tablespoons butter

2 1/2 cups turkey broth or 2 1/2 cups chicken broth

**Method:**

Saute the onions, celery. herbs, salt and pepper in butter until soft.

Combine with the bread cubes by tossing lightly, adding enough broth to

the bird.

it in a baking dish covered with foil or a lid, and continue to bake
outside of the bird until it is at that temp. (food safety)

*Todd*

Salt

Beef rib roast (or rib eye roast)\*

Thaw roast. Rub a generous amount of salt, pepper and seasoned salt into
the roast. Place roast, fat side up, on a rack in an open roasting pan.
If using a bone-in roast, the ribs will form the rack that keeps the
meat above the drippings. Insert a meat thermometer in to the thickest
part of the roast, not touching bone or fat. Do NOT add water. Do NOT
cover.

Roast in oven at 350 to 325 degrees until meat thermometer registers 5
to 10 degrees below desired doneness. During the standing time, roast
will continue to rise 5 to 10 degrees and reach the final meat
thermometer reading. (Oven does not have to be preheated.)

Doneness Min. / lb. Internal Temp

Rare 17 to 19 135 to 140

Med-Rare 20 to 22 145 to 150

Medium 23 to 25 155 to160

Well-done 27 to 30 165 to 170

Allow the roast to stand tented with foil for 15 to 20 minutes before
serving. Temperature will rise, and roast will be easier to carve.

per pound; bone-in roasts yield about two 3-oz. cooked, trimmed servings

**High Altitude Baking Tips**

\* Use all-purpose flour instead of cake flour for better consistency.

\* Have all baking ingredients at room temperature before starting.

\* Reduce sugar by 2 Tbl for every 1 cup called for to help strengthen
cookie or cake batter.

\* Over 2,500 feet, do not beat egg whites to stiff peaks. Stop when
whites have soft droopy peaks -- this gives room for expansion.

\* Avoid quick or rapid-rise yeast when making bread. It can make dough
rise too rapidly, affecting texture and flavor.

\* When making quick breads, use buttermilk or yogurt. They will help
the batter set up more quickly and prevent sunken centers.

\* Fill muffin cups closer to the rim when making muffins.

\* When making candy, remember that water boils at lower temperatures at
higher elevations (about 2 degrees per 1,000 feet above sea level.) Test
boiling point and adjust recipes.

**Cakes**

Cakes will rise quicker and fall quicker with lower air pressure. The
following can help strengthen batter: Add less sugar or sweeteners; add
a whole egg or extra egg yolk; add an additional 1-2 Tbl flour or other
starch; decrease leavening by ¼-1/8 tsp.

**Cookies**

Batter can spread too much, making cookies thin and tough. To prevent
this, try the following:

Raise the oven temp up to 25 degrees and cut back baking time; add a bit
more flour or reduce sugar; or reduce leavening.

**Muffins**

Fill cups to the rim (test this -- 2/3 full may be enough). Above 3,00
feet, you may need to increase temp and baking time slightly (but do not
overcook!)

**Quick Breads**

Reduce leavening slightly; add extra liquid, such as using buttermilk or
yogurt (see tips at the top.)

**Yeast Breads**

Low pressure can cause rising to happen too quickly, affecting texture
and flavor. To prevent this, punch dough down more often (at least three
times) and avoid instant or rapid yeast. You can also refrigerate dough
overnight. If dough tends to collapse, reduce the amount of sugar, but
add more salt to improve flavor and slow growth of yeast.
